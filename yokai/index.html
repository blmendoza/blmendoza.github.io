About the Project

I started to create this project "DigiYō" to educate others about Japanese yōkai through interactive encounters in a digital space. I chose this theme of yōkai because of the bizarre quality of specificity of a unique entity yet unspecificity of origin. It's interesting to me how one yōkai can have various interpretations and forms to fit different narratives.

What Are Yōkai?

Yōkai (妖怪) refer to spirits, monsters, and other supernatural creatures from Japanese folklore. The oldest record of yōkai could be seen as early as 8th century within Japan's creation myths. Yōkai saw a surge of popularity during the Edo period (1603-1868) when culture and art were flourishing. Similar to the creation myths, yōkai in the Edo period were used to help explain strange phenomenon from all over the country.


Yōkai can possess animal features, human-like features, appear as inanimate objects, or have no discernible shape at all. Most usually have spiritual or supernatural abilities, with shapeshifting being the most common. Heavily influenced by the concept of animism (the belief that objects, places and creatures all possess a distinct spiritual essence and thus have some degree of agency, along with emotions and personalities), yōkai can be considered peaceful, bringers of good fortune, bringers of ill-fortune, or violent.
